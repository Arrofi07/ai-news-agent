Adoption and Ecosystem Health: A Longitudinal Analysis of Open-Source Multi-Agent Frameworks

arXiv · Score: 58/100

A longitudinal analysis of 15 open-source multi-agent frameworks reveals that traditional popularity metrics like GitHub stars are unreliable indicators of ecosystem health. Instead, contributor density, cross-ecosystem engagement (e.g., LangChain as shared infrastructure), and contributor retention are more robust measures for evaluating framework adoption and sustainability. The study highlights that high visibility doesn't always translate to deep community engagement.

WattGPU: Predicting Inference Power and Latency on Unseen GPUs and LLMs

arXiv · Score: 58/100

WattGPU introduces two predictive models for mean GPU power draw and Inter-Token Latency (ITL) during LLM inference. These models leverage only publicly available LLM metadata and GPU specifications, eliminating the need for hardware profiling and enabling generalization to unseen NVIDIA server-grade GPUs and LLMs. It achieves significantly lower prediction errors and strong GPU ranking correlations compared to standard baselines, particularly for unseen hardware combinations.
