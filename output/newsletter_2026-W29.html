WILDTRACE: Benchmarking Natural Evidence Trails in Long-Context Reasoning

arXiv · Score: 62/100

WILDTRACE is a new benchmark designed to evaluate long-context reasoning in LLMs by focusing on naturally dispersed evidence within real-world documents like incident reports and literary narratives. Unlike existing benchmarks that use artificial evidence, WILDTRACE features 481 tasks over 214 sources, where evidence trails are inherent to the document's causal, temporal, and narrative logic. It defines seven source-internal evidence geometries and employs a rigorous multi-stage validation process to ensure clue necessity, answer groundedness, and contamination resistance.

💡 For AI and Data Engineers, WILDTRACE is crucial as it provides a more realistic evaluation of LLMs' ability to perform complex analytical tasks over long documents, directly impacting the reliability and performance of RAG systems and other data-intensive applications. This benchmark highlights the need for models that can genuinely reason over naturally integrated evidence, moving beyond simple information retrieval.
